About

This app seeks to make the large corpus of COVID-19 research easily parseable and available to researchers and curious individuals alike. There are two features: the COVID-19 ReSearch Engine and the Random Insights Generator. This app uses data pulled from the CORD-19 research challenge on Kaggle.

The ReSearch Engine uses data from the CORD-19 research challenge on Kaggle. This data contains information about literature pertaining to the novel coronavirus pandemic. The ReSearch Engine utilizes cosine similarity to rank the results in relation to the user query, with the ultimate goal of helping researchers locate relevant papers quickly. The results include the full abstract, cord uid, and paper title.

The Random Insight Generator (RIG) uses a subset of abstracts that are written in a specific form with a clearly delineated conclusion sentence. The RIG pulls ten random conclusion sentences as well as their corresponding paper title and cord uid. This part of the app can help curious individuals who want to know what scientists are doing with regard to COVID-19 jump into their own personal quests for factual, scientific evidence. Users can also use the RIG to find topics of interest, then navigate over to the ReSearch Engine to look up papers on the same subject.

All source code for this project can be found on Github.
